How protection travels

Your device → .sshot → Any channel

Encryption happens before upload. Sharing transfers access without passing passwords. The service handles sync and delivery, not plaintext access.

A different security model

Per-file protection, not a single vault door

Most apps secure a gallery behind one unlock. SecureShot protects each file independently, so opening one item does not expose the rest of your library.

Not a vault Each file is independently encrypted. Protection is defined by the .sshot file, not by a hidden folder or locked gallery.
Stays encrypted anywhere Export, back up, or send files through email, cloud storage, or messaging apps — they remain protected outside SecureShot.
No password passing Secure sharing transfers access without sending passphrases through chat or email.
Access with intent Open only what you need. Pro features reduce repeated friction with biometrics, thumbnails, and Master Key workflows.

Technical foundation

Modern cryptography, transparent guarantees

SecureShot uses industry-standard cryptography to protect content on device and move access safely between users — without turning the product into a jargon exercise.

Payloads use XChaCha20-Poly1305. Passphrase and key derivation use Argon2id. Secure sharing uses Curve25519 with HKDF to re-wrap per-file keys — without re-encrypting the media payload.

Privacy stance

Private by design

SecureShot is built so the operator does not need plaintext access to your protected files.

We never see your files Encrypted content is protected on device before upload. The service stores ciphertext, not readable media.
We do not track your content Operational analytics can measure app health and coarse usage patterns without logging plaintext, filenames, handles, or secrets.
No hidden data harvesting SecureShot is not built around profiling your private content, scraping your media, or learning what your files are about.
Your keys stay with you Zero-knowledge here means the secrets required to decrypt protected files are not available to the service.
